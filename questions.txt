CORE DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

1. What is the causal question in this study, and is Mendelian randomisation an appropriate method to answer it?

2. Do the genetic instruments used in the study satisfy the relevance assumption? What evidence is provided?

3. Is the independence assumption likely to hold in this study? How do the authors address potential confounding?

4. How do the authors assess and address pleiotropy (exclusion restriction assumption)? Are their approaches sufficient?

5. How should we interpret the effect estimates from this MR study? What do they represent, and what do they NOT represent?


OPTIONAL / EXTENSION QUESTIONS

6. Using the Davies checklist, which MR assumptions are well supported in this paper, and which remain uncertain?

7. What would be a useful negative control in this study, and how could it strengthen causal inference?

8. Could weak instrument bias or sample overlap affect the results? How do the authors address this?

9. How do the MR results compare with the observational findings, and what does this tell us?

10. If MR and observational results differ, how should we interpret this discrepancy?
